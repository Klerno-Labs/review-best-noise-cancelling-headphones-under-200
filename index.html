Buyer’s Guide

Choosing the right pair of headphones can be overwhelming given the technical jargon and marketing hype. To help you choose the best noise cancelling headphones under 200, we have broken down the essential factors you need to consider.

Noise Cancellation Technology


The core feature you are paying for is Active Noise Cancellation (ANC). This technology uses microphones to pick up ambient sound waves and then uses a speaker to play the exact opposite sound wave, effectively cancelling the noise out. In this price range, look for headphones that support hybrid ANC, which combines active noise blocking with physical noise isolation (passive isolation from the ear cups). When reading reviews, look for headphones that offer multiple ANC modes, such as "Transport Mode" found in the Anker Space Q45, which adjusts automatically to your environment.

Battery Life


Battery life is crucial, especially for travelers. Most good headphones in this bracket will offer between 30 to 60 hours of playtime with ANC on. Some models, like the soundcore Space One Pro, claim up to 60 hours, while others like the Sony WH-CH720N offer around 35 hours. Always check the battery life with ANC on versus off, as the latter is often a marketing number. Fast charging capabilities (like 5 minutes charge for several hours) are a life-saver if you are in a rush.

Comfort and Fit


Comfort is subjective, but fit is objective. The best headphones in the world are useless if they hurt your ears or pinch your head. Look for models with memory foam earpads and an adjustable headband. Over-ear models (like the Sennheiser Accentum) typically offer better passive noise isolation and comfort for long sessions than on-ear models. However, if you plan to wear them for extended periods, ensure the clamping force isn't too tight. For heads with smaller ears, on-ear models might pinch, so over-ear is generally safer for value buys.

Sound Quality


Sound quality in this price range has improved dramatically. You will generally find Balanced and Neutral sound profiles (like the Sennheiser models) which are great for mixing and critical listening, or Bass-boosted profiles (like JBL and Sony ULT WEAR) which are fun for pop and electronic music. Check the frequency response and driver size (usually 40mm is standard). A good app ecosystem is also a plus, as it allows you to customize EQ settings to your liking.

Portability


If you travel frequently, portability is a key factor. Some headphones, like the soundcore Space One Pro, are designed to be compact and foldable, making them easy to pack. Others, while offering great ANC, are bulkier. Consider the weight of the device; lighter headphones are easier to carry around all day. Check if a hard case is included, though most budget models ship with a soft carry pouch.

FAQ

What is active noise cancellation?
Active noise cancellation (ANC) is a technology that uses microphones to pick up external sound waves and then produces sound waves of the exact opposite phase (sound inversion) to cancel them out. This results in a quieter listening environment, effectively silencing the hum of an airplane engine or the rumble of a train.

How long do the batteries last?
Most of the best noise cancelling headphones under 200 on this list offer between 35 and 60 hours of battery life. For example, the JBL Live 460NC and soundcore Space One Pro boast very long runtimes. However, battery life varies based on whether ANC is turned on, the volume level, and the codec being used for audio transmission.

Are these headphones comfortable for long use?
Comfort is a strong point for many of these models. The Sennheiser Accentum and Edifier WH950NB are specifically noted for "all-day comfort" and memory foam earpads. However, models like the JBL Live 460NC have cloth headbands that may feel less padded for users with sensitive heads, so it is important to read specific comfort reviews.

Can I use them for phone calls?
Yes, all the headphones listed are Bluetooth-enabled and include built-in microphones. The Edifier WH950NB and Anker Soundcore Space Q45 are particularly highlighted for having clear and impressive microphone performance, making them suitable for conference calls and voice messaging.

Do they come with a warranty?
Most major brands like Sony, Sennheiser, JBL, and Anker provide a standard manufacturer's warranty (usually 1 year) on their headphones. We recommend checking the specific warranty terms on the product page at the time of purchase to ensure you are covered for any defects.
