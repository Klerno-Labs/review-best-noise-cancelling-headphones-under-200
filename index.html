Buyer's Guide

Noise Cancellation Technology


Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) is the most critical feature when looking for the best noise cancelling headphones under 200. ANC uses microphones to detect external noise and produces an inverse sound wave to cancel it out. In this price range, you will typically find Hybrid ANC (using two microphones) or Standard ANC.
  • Hybrid ANC: Uses an outer microphone to detect noise and an inner microphone to adjust the cancellation. This is the industry standard for good sound quality and noise cancellation.
  • Standard ANC: Uses a single microphone. It is generally effective for low-frequency noise (like engines) but may struggle with high-pitched sounds (like human voices).
  • Transport Mode: Look for headphones with specialized modes for planes or trains, like the Anker Soundcore Space Q45. These modes optimize the ANC specifically for engine rumble, often providing the best isolation in noisy environments.

Battery Life


Battery life determines how often you need to charge your device. For best noise cancelling headphones under 200, look for models offering at least 30 hours of playback with ANC on.
  • Fast Charging: Many of the top models in this list include fast charging. A 10-minute charge can often provide several hours of playback, which is crucial for travel.
  • Charging Ports: USB-C is now the standard. Ensure the headphones support USB-C charging, as it is more convenient than micro-USB.

Comfort and Fit


Wearing headphones for long periods requires them to be comfortable. If the headphones are too tight, you will experience headaches.
  • Ear Cup Material: Memory foam ear pads are a must-have for comfort. They mold to your ear shape, distributing pressure evenly.
  • Headband Padding: Leatherette is standard, but cloth or padded leather can be more breathable. Be aware that cloth bands, like those on the JBL Live 460NC, can sometimes be uncomfortable for glasses wearers.
  • Weight: Lightweight headphones are easier to carry. The Sony WH-CH720N is a great example of a lightweight option.

Sound Quality


Sound quality encompasses frequency response, drivers, and codec support.
  • Frequency Response: Decide if you prefer a "V-shape" (bass-heavy) sound or a "flat" (natural) sound. JBL headphones typically offer punchy bass, while Sennheiser models often provide a more balanced sound.
  • Codecs: Look for support for modern codecs like AAC and SBC. Some higher-end models in this range support aptX, which can improve audio quality over Bluetooth for Android users.

Portability


If you travel frequently, the physical size and weight of the headphones matter.
  • Foldable Design: Foldable headphones save space in your bag. The soundcore Space One Pro is specifically praised for its compact folding design.
  • Case: Many headphones come with a carrying case. While not always mandatory, a hard case offers better protection for your investment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is active noise cancellation?


Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) is a technology that reduces unwanted ambient sound using external sound waves. The headphones contain microphones that pick up environmental noise. An internal processor generates an opposing sound wave (anti-noise) to cancel out the incoming noise. This results in a quieter listening experience, making these headphones the best noise cancelling headphones under 200 for commuting.

How long do the batteries last?


Battery life varies by model, but most headphones in this list offer between 30 and 60 hours of playback time. For example, the soundcore Space One Pro offers up to 60 hours with ANC on, while the Sony WH-CH720N offers 35 hours. Additionally, most models feature fast charging, allowing for several hours of use from just a few minutes of charging.

Are these headphones comfortable for long use?


Comfort is a major consideration for any headphone purchase. Most models in this list, such as the Sennheiser ACCENTUM Plus and Edifier WH950NB, are designed with memory foam earpads and adjustable headbands to ensure all-day comfort. However, users with sensitive heads or glasses might find the cloth headband on models like the JBL Live 460NC slightly less comfortable than the leatherette alternatives.

Can I use them for phone calls?


Yes, all of the headphones listed feature built-in microphones, which are great for making and taking calls. Many models, including the Anker Soundcore Space Q45 and Edifier WH950NB, have specifically been praised for their microphone performance in noisy environments, ensuring your voice is clear to the person on the other end.

Do they come with a warranty?


Yes, most major brands, including Anker, Sennheiser, Sony, and JBL, offer a standard manufacturer's warranty that covers defects in materials and workmanship for a specified period (usually one year). It is always recommended to register your product with the manufacturer after purchase to activate any extended warranty benefits or support services.
